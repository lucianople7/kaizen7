# NEUROCITY

## Working title

**NEUROCITY — an original series by THE FOCUX**

`FOCUX INSIDE` remains a campaign line, not the master title, pending trademark review. `NEUROCITY` also requires trademark and domain clearance before registration or merchandising.

## One-line premise

Inside every demanding mind is a city that never stops. FOCUX and its operating team explain what happens when attention, memory, stress, sleep and energy compete for control.

## Strategic role

NEUROCITY is not an advertisement for supplements. It is THE FOCUX's educational entertainment franchise.

Its job is to create:

- Memory structures around complex ideas.
- Original characters and recurring narrative tension.
- A recognisable visual language.
- Trust before commercial recommendations.
- A bridge from entertainment to evidence-led dossiers.

## Audience

Primary:

- Professionals, founders, programmers and executives aged 30-60.
- Interested in focus, sleep, stress, energy and cognitive performance.
- Intelligent but not necessarily scientifically trained.
- Allergic to childish motivation and biohacking hype.

Secondary:

- Health professionals and science communicators.
- Younger adults reached through short-form video.

## Tone

- Adult animated editorial thriller.
- Calm, intelligent and visually precise.
- Dry humour, not slapstick.
- Tension without fearmongering.
- Optimistic without promising optimisation.
- Scientific humility is part of the drama.

## Visual direction

### Keep

- Futuristic city metaphor.
- Black, graphite and restrained metallic gold.
- Functional districts.
- Strong silhouettes and recurring symbols.
- Controlled accent colour for each character.

### Change from the concept image

- No chibi or children's-film proportions.
- FOCUX must feel calm, not threatening.
- No glowing angry eyes.
- Characters should look like adult graphic-novel animation, not toys.
- Gold identifies system signals and verified information, not magic.
- Accent colours remain muted and occupy less than 15% of frame.
- Architecture should be functional, inspectable and readable on mobile.
- No claims such as “maximum potential”, “restores balance” or “keeps the brain at maximum performance”.

### Rendering system

- Cinematic 2.5D or stylised 3D.
- Real materials: matte black metal, smoked glass, stone, paper and controlled light.
- 16:9 master scene.
- 9:16 safe composition for vertical adaptation.
- 1:1 character portraits.
- Stable character sheets before animation.

## The city

NEUROCITY is not a literal anatomical map. It is a teaching metaphor. Every episode states where the metaphor stops.

### Core districts

#### Executive Tower

Represents planning, inhibition, task selection and working control. It is inspired by executive networks, not a single “focus centre”.

#### Memory District

Archives, indexing rooms and reconstruction studios. Memories are not static books; Memo rebuilds them when they are retrieved.

#### Signal Exchange

A station for sensory traffic, salience and interruption. Notifications arrive here before competing for access to Executive Tower.

#### Reward Market

Dopi's territory. It tracks prediction, salience, motivation and learning. It is not a simple pleasure district.

#### Alarm Network

Corti coordinates acute response. Essential during genuine demands, damaging when the emergency protocol never stands down.

#### Night Shift

Maintenance, memory processing and metabolic housekeeping during sleep. Avoid stating that sleep “cleans toxins” as a settled universal claim.

#### Energy Grid

Mitochondrial and systemic energy metaphor. It cannot create attention by itself; it supplies conditions for work.

## Core cast

### FOCUX — The Conductor

Role: protagonist and systems coordinator.

FOCUX does not possess superpowers and never “fixes” the brain. FOCUX asks the right question, slows the city down and gets competing systems to communicate.

Personality:

- Quiet authority.
- Precise speech.
- Curious before decisive.
- Comfortable saying “we do not know yet”.

Visual:

- Tall, human-like silhouette.
- Matte black tailored technical coat.
- Thin gold ring as interface, not halo.
- Warm neutral eyes or visor; never aggressive.
- Minimal movement and controlled gestures.

Voice:

- Calm, low and unhurried.
- No motivational shouting.

### DOPI — The Signal Broker

Represents: dopamine systems in motivation, prediction, salience and learning.

Not allowed: “dopamine is pleasure” or “dopamine detox resets your brain”.

Personality:

- Fast, persuasive and opportunistic.
- Loves novelty and prediction errors.
- Can sustain difficult work when the reward is meaningful.
- Creates problems when every signal looks urgent.

Visual:

- Compact, agile silhouette.
- Muted amber accent.
- Carries signal tokens whose value changes with expectation.

### CORTI — The Alarm Chief

Represents: stress response and cortisol as one part of a larger adaptive system.

Not a villain. Corti saves the city during real emergencies. The conflict appears when acute protocol becomes chronic operating mode.

Personality:

- Responsible, intense and literal.
- Hates uncertainty.
- Escalates because nobody gives the all-clear.
- Learns recovery and proportional response.

Visual:

- Angular silhouette.
- Muted deep red accent.
- Emergency jacket, not demon or fire creature.

### MEMO — The Archivist

Represents: encoding, consolidation, retrieval and reconstruction.

Personality:

- Methodical and imaginative.
- Knows that memory is reconstructed, not replayed.
- Needs attention during encoding and sleep during maintenance.
- Sometimes confidently retrieves the wrong version.

Visual:

- Indigo accent.
- Modular archive tools and movable index panels.
- Adult proportions, practical clothing.

### NEURO — The Investigator

Represents: scientific method, evidence and uncertainty.

Role:

- Breaks the fourth wall.
- Separates metaphor from biology.
- Shows evidence grade and study limitations.
- Corrects the other characters without humiliating them.

Visual:

- Muted mineral green accent.
- Modern field researcher, not eccentric old scientist.
- Carries a transparent evidence slate.

## Recurring guest systems

- **ADENO:** sleep pressure and the accumulating cost of wakefulness.
- **GLIA:** infrastructure, support and maintenance; avoids the myth that neurons work alone.
- **MITO:** energy-grid engineer; explains that “energy” is not a single feeling or molecule.
- **SERA:** mood and behavioural flexibility; avoids “serotonin equals happiness”.

Only one guest enters an episode unless the story requires more.

## Scientific guardrails

Every episode must contain:

1. A defined learning objective.
2. A metaphor statement.
3. A “where the metaphor breaks” line.
4. Primary or authoritative sources.
5. Evidence grade.
6. Safety and uncertainty where relevant.
7. Medical review for clinical, medication or peptide topics.

Forbidden shortcuts:

- One molecule equals one emotion.
- One brain region equals one function.
- Hormone or neurotransmitter as hero/villain.
- “Hack”, “reset”, “detox” or “maximum potential” as scientific conclusions.
- Guaranteed cognitive improvement.
- Individual treatment or dosing protocol.
- Supplement placement disguised as education.

## Episode grammar

### Flagship episode: 5-8 minutes

1. Cold open in the real world: 10-20 seconds.
2. Incident enters NeuroCity.
3. A character applies the wrong simple explanation.
4. Systems conflict becomes visible.
5. Neuro performs the reality check.
6. FOCUX coordinates a response.
7. One practical action or decision rule.
8. Evidence grade and sources.
9. Tease for the next episode.

### Vertical episode: 45-75 seconds

- One tension.
- One metaphor.
- One correction.
- One useful decision.
- CTA to dossier, not product.

### Micro-content: 12-20 seconds

- Dopi interrupts.
- Corti escalates a harmless signal.
- Memo reconstructs something incorrectly.
- Neuro corrects a viral myth.

## Brand and commercial rules

- Season 1 contains no embedded product placement.
- Ingredients enter only after the audience understands the system.
- Commercial products never appear as characters.
- A sponsored episode must be labelled before playback and cannot change the conclusion.
- The editorial dossier and product page remain separate URLs.
- Characters can lead to commerce only through an evidence page.
