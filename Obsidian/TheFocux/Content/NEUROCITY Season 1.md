# NEUROCITY — Season 1

## Season promise

Ten episodes explaining why attention, memory, energy, stress and sleep behave differently from the simple stories sold online.

## Production order

### 1. The Notification Siege

Question: why can a tiny notification break serious work?

- District: Signal Exchange and Executive Tower.
- Lead: Dopi.
- Correction: distraction is not just weak willpower or “too much dopamine”.
- Action: reduce competing cues and define a visible next action.
- Dossier: attention switching, salience and interruption cost.

### 2. When Corti Never Clocks Out

Question: why can stress help for an hour and hurt for a month?

- District: Alarm Network.
- Lead: Corti.
- Correction: cortisol is adaptive, not toxic by definition.
- Action: distinguish emergency, demand and recovery.
- Dossier: acute vs chronic stress and measurement limits.

### 3. The Night Shift

Question: what actually fails after insufficient sleep?

- District: Night Shift and Memory District.
- Guest: Adeno.
- Correction: sleep is not simply “turning off” or a universal detox.
- Action: protect wake time, sleep opportunity and regularity.
- Dossier: sleep pressure, attention and memory.

### 4. Memo's Missing File

Question: why do we forget something we just read?

- District: Memory Archive.
- Lead: Memo.
- Correction: exposure is not encoding; memory is reconstructive.
- Action: retrieval practice and spacing.
- Dossier: encoding, consolidation and retrieval.

### 5. The Multitasking Heist

Question: are we really doing two demanding tasks at once?

- District: Executive Tower.
- Leads: Dopi and FOCUX.
- Correction: task switching can feel productive while adding cost.
- Action: one cognitive target per work block.
- Dossier: task-switching evidence and exceptions.

### 6. The Empty Energy Gauge

Question: does feeling tired always mean the brain lacks fuel?

- District: Energy Grid.
- Guest: Mito.
- Correction: perceived energy has sleep, metabolic, motivational and clinical dimensions.
- Action: rule out foundations before adding stimulants.
- Dossier: fatigue as a multi-system signal.

### 7. Caffeine Enters the City

Question: what does caffeine change, and what does it borrow?

- District: Signal Exchange and Night Shift.
- Guest: Adeno.
- Correction: caffeine does not create sleep; it changes perception of sleep pressure.
- Action: timing and individual context before dose escalation.
- Dossier: caffeine, half-life, sleep and performance.

### 8. The Calm Signal

Question: why is L-theanine commonly paired with caffeine?

- District: Signal Exchange.
- Lead: Neuro.
- Correction: combination evidence is not proof of universal benefit.
- Action: inspect population, outcome and exact formula.
- Dossier: L-theanine + caffeine evidence map.

### 9. Creatine Beyond the Gym

Question: why is creatine discussed in cognition?

- District: Energy Grid.
- Guest: Mito.
- Correction: emerging evidence differs from an authorised commercial health claim.
- Action: separate scientific discussion, product claim and personal decision.
- Dossier: creatine cognition meta-analysis and EU claim status.

### 10. The Miracle Stack

Question: how can a 14-ingredient label say almost nothing?

- District: Research Court.
- Full cast.
- Correction: ingredient count does not equal efficacy.
- Action: dose, form, evidence, safety and COA checklist.
- Dossier: THE FOCUX Buyer Standard.

## Release cadence

- One flagship episode every two weeks.
- Two vertical adaptations per episode.
- Three micro-clips per episode.
- One newsletter and one evidence dossier per episode.
- Season duration: 20 weeks.

## Pilot gate

Produce episodes 1, 2 and 3 as an initial arc. Continue only if:

- 35% of viewers reach halfway on flagship video.
- 8% save or share vertical content.
- 3% click to evidence dossier.
- 5% of dossier visitors join the newsletter.
- Scientific review finds no material correction.
